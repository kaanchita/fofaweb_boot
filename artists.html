"GEORGE

BOLSTER"

New York City based artist George Bolster graduated from Central Saint Martins School of Art and Design in London with an MA in Fine Art in 1999. His multidisciplinary practice -through oppositional narratives- addresses ideas and belief systems from multiple perspectives. In films such as Un/natural History: Drowning Captiva (addressing climate change, rising sea levels and Rauschenberg's environmental legacy) and The Moon, McMoon’s, and the Moon Museum (focuses on the first art object in space, and long-term cultural conservation in the face of environmental disintegration). He utilizes a combination of science, art history, and sci-fi to examine our most prescient societal and species-wide challenges. Bolster’s work has been exhibited in numerous museums and galleries in Europe, America, Korea and Canada, including MASS MoCA, Massachusetts, USA; Sharjah Art Foundation, Sharjah, UAE; RHA, Dublin, Ireland; CCI, Paris, France; and MMCA, Seoul, Korea. The artist is the recipient of residencies at CCI, Paris, France (2019); Elizabeth Foundation for The Arts, New York, USA (2017); SETI Institute, Mountain View, California (2016-17); the Robert Rauschenberg Residency, Captiva, Florida, USA (2013); and The Firestation Residency, Dublin, Ireland (2012). Bolster has been awarded numerous grants from the Arts Council of Ireland, Culture Ireland and the Arts Council of England. Bolster’s monograph When Will We Recognize Us was recently published by Hirmer Verlag, and his work has featured in Artforum, The New York Times, Washington Post, Irish Times and Irish Art Review. He is currently Curator for the Shelley & Donald Rubin Foundation in NYC.
